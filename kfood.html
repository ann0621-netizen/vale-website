OUR MENU

CHOOSE & ENJOY

The food we offer in the Philippines stays true to the authentic recipes, ingredients, and cooking methods we use in Korea. Our menu features a wide range of flavors, including traditional favorites and exciting new creations.

Jjangmyeon

Noodle dish topped with a thick sauce made of chunjang, diced pork, and vegetables

Samgyaetang

Whole young chicken or quail filled with garlic, rice, jujube and ginseng.

Naengmyeon

Long and thin handmade noodles made from the flour and starch of various ingredients, including buckwheat, potatoes, sweet potatoes, arrowroot starch, and kudzu.

Beef Bulgogi

Gui made of thin, marinated slices of meat, most commonly beef, grilled on a barbecue or on a stove-top griddle.

Kongguksu

Noodles in cold soybean soup is a seasonal Korean noodle dish served in a cold soy milk broth.

Japchae

Cellophane noodles made from sweet potato starch; the noodles are mixed with assorted vegetables, meat, and mushrooms, and seasoned with soy sauce and sesame oil.

Gamjatang

Pork back-bone stew is a spicy Korean soup made from the spine or neck bones of a pig.

Hobakjuk

Pumpkin porridge, or juk, made with pumpkin and glutinous rice flour

Sundubu-jjigae

Freshly curdled extra soft tofu which has not been strained and pressed, vegetables, sometimes mushrooms, onion, optional seafood, optional meat, and gochujang.
